Key Concepts

The Problem: Student Blind Spots. We'll briefly review these problems and how to solve them.

  • High attrition rates from math-intensive STEM classes
  • Poor ability to solve real-world problems
  • Poor conceptual understanding (basic misconceptions on 50% of the concepts, and little improvement by the end of a course)
  • Missing ways to identify specific misconceptions, or to see how to fix them
  • Instructors lack a way to see what each student is actually thinking, and thus cannot diagnose their underlying conceptual errors, let alone fix them
